Monroe County and Bloomington city government are planning the construction of a new, bigger jail.

The county is about to buy 87 acres of land ($10+ million) by Fullerton Pike, and build a large facility ($50-60 million), with more construction in the future. They're also talking about building a new juvie there.

They plan to use money from the recent income tax increase to fund the project.

Before spending $70 million, we think the County should think things through.

  1. What would it cost to fix the current jail? No serious cost estimates have been done, and it doesn't make sense to move forward without this information.

  2. What steps can they take to have fewer people in jail?

"If you build it, they're gonna fill it."

—— Sam Crowe, Jail Commander (Sept 19th 2022, Monroe County Community Justice Response Committee)

Local addiction & mental health services, (especially grassroots organizations that don't work closely with the police) do not have enough funding. The cost of living is quickly getting more expensive.

The bottom line is that putting struggling people in cages, and branding them with a criminal record to cut them off from housing and employment, makes life harder for everyone.

"...reduce incarceration by implementing non- law-enforcement crisis interventions [and] alternatives to incarceration."

"...jail is never going to be an effective mental health treatment provider and should not be relied upon as such."

—— 2021 Ray-JCI study, the latest criminal justice recommendations commissioned by the county

We don't have to do this.

The solutions to "crime" are already here, and just need the real financial and legal support of people in power.
